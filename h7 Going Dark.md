Quintin 2014: 7 Things You Should Know About Tor
Tor still works: 
Despite NSA leaks, the core encryption and design of Tor remain secure against most global surveillance
Tor is for everyone: 
It is not just for criminals; it is used by journalists, activists, and ordinary people seeking privacy
Anonymity is not a magic bullet: 
Tor hides your location, but user errors (like logging into personal accounts) can still deanonymize you
No "backdoors": 
Tor is open-source and developed by a non-profit, meaning its code is transparent and lacks hidden government access

Shavers & Bair 2016: Hiding Behind the Keyboard: The Tor Browser €; subchapters: "Introduction", "History and Intended Use of The Onion Router", "How The Onion Router Works", "Tracking Criminals Using TOR"
Introduction: 
Tor is a modified Firefox browser designed to hide a user's IP address, making tracking extremely difficult for investigators
History and Intended Use: 
Originally developed by the US Navy, its goal is to allow unfettered, anonymous communication for whistleblowers and citizens under oppressive regimes
How The Onion Router Works: 
It uses "onion routing" where data is layered in encryption and passed through entry, middle, and exit relays to strip identity
Tracking Criminals Using Tor: 
Law enforcement focuses on forensic artifacts left on the physical device or "side-channel" attacks rather than breaking the Tor network itself
